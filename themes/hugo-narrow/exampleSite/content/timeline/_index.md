This demo shows how the timeline template turns each `##` heading into a timeline block while keeping the writing experience in plain Markdown.

## Initial planning

We started by defining the scope, collecting references, and outlining the user experience we wanted from the theme.

- Clarify the reading flow
- Decide the visual direction
- Keep content authoring simple

## First release with *core layout*

The first public version focused on a clean reading experience, responsive layout, and a flexible content structure.

> A small, stable foundation made later refinements much easier.

## Search and content tooling

As the theme grew, we added search, enhanced content components, and richer page templates for different kinds of writing.

You can also keep inline Markdown like **bold text**, `code`, and [links](https://gohugo.io/) in timeline headings and content.

## Refinement and polishing

The current iteration focuses on details such as heading anchors, image handling, multilingual support, and more intentional examples for theme users.
